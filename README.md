# Financial Modeling: DCF Valuation of CG Power & Industrial Solutions Ltd.

A comprehensive **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** valuation model developed from scratch to estimate the intrinsic value of **CG Power & Industrial Solutions Ltd.** using publicly available financial statements and established corporate finance methodologies.

---

## Overview

This project demonstrates the complete process of valuing a publicly listed company using the **Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)** approach.

The financial model was developed in **Microsoft Excel** by extracting data from the FY2024–25 Annual Report and applying industry-standard valuation techniques to estimate the company's intrinsic value.

---

## Objectives

- Analyze historical financial statements.
- Forecast future Free Cash Flows to Firm (FCFF).
- Estimate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC).
- Calculate Enterprise Value and Equity Value.
- Determine the Intrinsic Share Price using DCF valuation.

## Valuation Workflow

### Financial Data Collection

Financial information was manually extracted from the FY2024–25 Annual Report.

### FCFF Estimation

The model estimates Free Cash Flow to Firm using:

```
FCFF = EBIT × (1 − Tax Rate)
       + Depreciation
       − Capital Expenditure
       − Change in Working Capital
```

### Cost of Capital

The discount rate was calculated using:

- CAPM for Cost of Equity
- Cost of Debt
- Capital Structure Weights

to estimate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC).

### Cash Flow Forecasting

Future FCFF was projected and discounted using the calculated WACC.

### Terminal Value

Terminal Value was estimated using the Gordon Growth Model.

### Enterprise Valuation

Enterprise Value, Equity Value, and Intrinsic Share Price were derived from the discounted cash flows.
